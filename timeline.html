What exactly does this project consist of? I realize that I've never really laid this out in one place on this website in detail before. To rectify this, here is a very rough, roughly chronological list of things I have done and plan to do as part of this project:
  • Summer: Did some general planning. I've wanted to build a CNC for a while, but it wasn't really until this summer that I started putting together a concrete plan to do it for my senior year of homeschooling.
  • September 6 - October 4: Learned the basics of HTML and CSS. I think this part of the project went quite well (though I did end up going for a very simple design for this werbsite considering how much I learned).
  • September 16 - October 14: Spec'd CNC and researched possible designs. I summarize my findings here: Choosing a CNC Design.
  • October 14 - 25: 3D printed and otherwise sourced parts for the CNC build.
  • November 9: CNC build day! I kicked off the CNC assembly, making 3 of the 4 major subassemblies of the CNC.
  • November 23: Built a permanent table for the CNC, mostly out of 2x4s.
  • November 30: Finished building the CNC, mounted it to the table, and got it moving! At this point, nothing is dialed in or accurate.
  • December 7: Mounted the tool, took a first pass at dialing it in and squaring everything, and cut my first parts: a couple plates to improve the X axis beam's rigidity! I know that this squaring will be temporary, so I did it pretty quick and dirty; the machine is currently accurate to about the nearest 1/32".
  • December: Square, align, and otherwise dial in the CNC as much as possible. I would love to get to the point where I can cut joinery with this machine, but I think that it's going to be very hard to get that level of precision.
  • Late December - January: Make various test parts to get familiar with CAM and test the limits of the CNC. Also start planning (e.g. CADing) my spring semester project.
  • Spring semester: Build some significant project (maybe a trebuchet?) with the new CNC. Also maybe-probably upgrade the CNC in some way, depending on what I like and dislike about it.
