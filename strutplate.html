("CNC Rug Shop" is an play on an inside joke, and now also my machine's provisional name.)

I used Estlcam as my CAM software and, although cutting this one part only scratched the surface of what CAM can do, I was happy with my experience with Estlcam and plan to continue using it for at least my next few cuts, and quite possibly the forseeable future, if I don't find any software I like better.

I haven't yet installed a full-sized spoilboard or put a lot of time into optimizing dimensional accuracy (though the dimensional accuracy I have attained has been better than I expected, considering the amount of time I've spent). My next step is to mount the part I've cut to the CNC itself: it's a plate that'll run along the X axis and hopefully provide increased stabilityPart of the reason I haven't yet spent a ton of time dialing in the CNC is because installing this plate is going to stiffen everything up..
